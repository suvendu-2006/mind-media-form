🎓 Speaking Style Masterclass

Read this section once before practicing. These rules will make you sound like a pro.

🗣️ Voice Control

  • Pace: Speak at 70% of your normal speed. Slow = confident.
  • Volume: Start at medium. Drop to near-whisper for scary stats. Rise for punchlines.
  • Pitch: Lower your pitch for serious facts. Higher for jokes and energy.
  • Emphasis: Bold words in the script = say them LOUDER and SLOWER.
  • Rule of 3: When listing 3 things ("doesn't knock, doesn't ask, doesn't care") — speed up each one slightly. Builds momentum.

🧍 Body Language

  • Hands: Keep them visible. Use them to count points, point at screen, gesture naturally.
  • Feet: Plant them. Don't sway or shift weight. Stand like a tree.
  • Eyes: Pick 3 spots in the room — left, centre, right. Rotate between them. Never stare at one person.
  • Point at screen: When referencing stats or charts, physically turn and point. Then turn back to audience.
  • Don't grip the podium: If there's a podium, step beside it. Open stance = confidence.

😄 Humor Delivery

  • Deadpan: The funniest lines work best when you DON'T laugh. Keep a straight face. Let them laugh.
  • Pause after jokes: Say the funny line → stop talking → wait 2-3 seconds. Silence = comedy gold.
  • Don't explain the joke: If nobody laughs, move on. Never say "that was supposed to be funny."
  • Self-aware humor: Lines like "we doomscroll too" work because you're laughing at yourself.
  • Callback humor: Referencing an earlier joke later (like "the Rulebook from earlier") makes you sound polished.

⏱️ Pacing & Pauses

  • Before a big stat: Pause 2 seconds. Then say the number. Then pause again.
  • After a quote: Let it hang in the air for 2-3 seconds before continuing.
  • Between slides: Don't rush. Click, breathe, look at audience, then start talking.
  • When you lose your place: Just say "So..." and pause. Nobody will notice.
  • Total time target: ~20-25 minutes for all 15 slides. Don't rush, don't drag.

⚡ Energy Map

  • Slide 1-2: Warm, friendly, calm (60% energy)
  • Slide 3-5: Informative, steady, building interest (70% energy)
  • Slide 6-9: Intense, passionate, this-is-the-scary-part (85% energy)
  • Slide 10-12: Peak intensity, slow and powerful (90% energy)
  • Slide 13: Hopeful, uplifting, we-can-fix-this (75% energy)
  • Slide 14: Fun, playful, interactive (80% energy)
  • Slide 15: Calm, sincere, memorable (65% energy)

🚫 Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Don't read the slide: The audience can read. YOU add the story behind it.
  • Don't memorize word-for-word: Know the IDEA of each slide. Talk naturally.
  • Don't apologize: Never say "sorry if this is boring" or "I'm not good at this."
  • Don't look at your phone/notes: Glance if needed, but talk to PEOPLE not paper.
  • Don't say "umm" or "like": Replace filler words with silence. Silence is powerful.
  • Don't rush the ending: The last 2 slides are your LEGACY. Slow down.

The Mind's Mechanics — Active Inference

Okay, this is the slide where we get a little bit into neuroscience. Don't panic — I promise it won't hurt.

So here's something wild. Your brain is not a camera. It's not just recording what's happening around you. It's actually a prediction machine — constantly guessing what's going to happen next, and updating itself when it's wrong.

Scientists call this the Active Inference Framework. We call it — your brain having trust issues.

There are three parts to it.

The Guesser — your brain is always making predictions. What you'll see, what you'll hear, what happens next. It's running a simulation of reality inside your head. All the time.

The Rulebook — this is everything your brain has learned about how the world works. Patterns, expectations. The blueprint it uses to make those guesses.

The Alarm — when reality doesn't match the prediction? Your brain throws an error. A "wait, that's not what I expected" moment. And that feeling is what drives your behaviour.

Now — social media is messing with all three of these. It's feeding your Rulebook fake data, keeping your Alarm ringing nonstop, and making your Guesser completely confused about what's real anymore.

The Goldilocks Zone of Uncertainty

So we just established that your brain's Alarm keeps firing every time something unexpected happens. Now here's the question — how much unexpected is too much?

This is where it gets fascinating. Your brain actually has a preference zone.

Scientists call it — The Goldilocks Zone of Uncertainty.

You know the story right? Porridge too hot, too cold, then just right. Your brain works exactly the same way with uncertainty.

Zone 1 — Too predictable. Everything is expected, nothing surprises you. Your brain checks out. Boring. You leave.

Zone 3 — Too unpredictable. Everything is chaotic, nothing makes sense. Your brain gets overwhelmed. Anxious. You also leave.

Zone 2 — The sweet spot. Just enough uncertainty to keep you curious. Just enough resolution to feel satisfied. And the moment you resolve one surprise — boom, another one appears.

That is where social media locks you. Permanently. By design.

And here's the really uncomfortable part — that bottom line on the slide.

Real life starts feeling like Zone 3 because of Zone 2 overload.

Meaning — after hours of perfectly engineered Zone 2 stimulation from your feed, your brain's tolerance goes up. Real life — which used to be perfectly fine — now feels too slow, too boring, too unpredictable in the wrong way. Like your brain forgot how to just... exist without stimulation.

Inside the Brain

The Prefrontal Cortex. This is the part that makes you a functioning adult. Focus, decision making, self control — all of this lives here. Every time dopamine hits from a like or a notification, this part gets a little weaker. A little more eroded.

And the worst part? You don't feel it happening. There's no pain. No warning. Just one day you realise you can't focus for more than two minutes, you forget things constantly, and every decision feels exhausting.

The Fix — Move, Mindful, Connect

Okay. After everything we just told you — the shrinking brain, the stress hormones, the 0% disclaimer effect — you're probably thinking one of two things.

Exercise. Walking consistently has been shown to reduce depression risk and actually rebuild the hippocampus we just said was shrinking. Your brain can recover. All it needs is for you to go outside. Which is free. Which is crazy.

Mindfulness and detox. Even a two-week social media break produces dramatic improvements in mood, sleep, and focus. Two weeks. Not forever. Just long enough for your brain to remember what normal feels like.

Real human connection. Face to face. Actual conversations. The kind where you can't edit what you say before sending it. That's what builds real resilience — not followers, not likes.

Design reforms. Hiding like counts, making algorithms transparent, giving users actual control. Some platforms are starting. Not enough. But it's moving.
